Contemporary landscape

Today, a few major browsers dominate the market. Google Chrome leads on both desktop and mobile due to speed, extension ecosystem, and Google account integration. Safari is dominant on Apple platforms, optimized for energy efficiency. Firefox remains an option for privacy-focused users, while Microsoft Edge leverages Chromium and Windows integration.

Recent innovations highlighted in research

  • Cross-device syncing and continuity with tabs, history, and passwords.
  • Privacy protections, like tracker blocking and third-party cookie restrictions.
  • Progressive Web Apps (PWAs) and offline capabilities.
  • AI integration for summarization, conversational assistants, and workflow automation.

AI and browsers

AI-powered features are becoming more common, with features like page summarization, integrated chat assistants, and task automation inside the browser. These features aim to make browsing more efficient but also raise privacy questions depending on how data is processed.

Mobile & performance

Mobile browsers are optimized for resource constraints and energy consumption. Performance and battery life are competitive differentiators on smartphones.
